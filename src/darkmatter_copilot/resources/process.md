# Dark Matter Studio — Delivery Process

How a project moves from first contact to launch and beyond. Use this as
a reference when writing proposals or explaining how we work.

## Standard timeline: 3-6 weeks

Most projects ship in 3-6 weeks from signed proposal to launch. The exact
timeline depends on scope, client responsiveness, and content readiness.

## The seven stages

### 1. Discovery call (free, ~30 minutes)

Understand the client's business, their current site (if any), their goals,
their budget range. Decide if we're a fit. No commitment from either side.

### 2. Scoping document (within 2 business days)

Written document covering: project scope, deliverables, timeline, fixed fee,
what's included and what isn't. This becomes the proposal.

### 3. Proposal acceptance & deposit

50% deposit on signing. Project starts when deposit clears.

### 4. Design

Initial design concepts based on scoping doc. One round of revisions included
in base price. Additional rounds billed separately ($200/round).

### 5. Build

Development happens in Next.js + Tailwind, deployed to Vercel for preview.
Client gets a live preview URL throughout the build.

### 6. Review & refinement

Client reviews the staging site. One round of revisions included. We iterate
to closure.

### 7. Launch

Domain pointed, site live, final balance due. Post-launch checklist completed:
- Analytics installed and verified
- SEO basics in place (sitemap, robots.txt, meta tags)
- Forms tested
- Mobile responsiveness verified
- Performance audit passed

## Post-launch support

30 days of post-launch support included in every project — minor bug fixes,
small content tweaks, anything broken or off.

After 30 days, ongoing support is available via retainer (see pricing).

## Communication

- **Primary channel:** email for substantive decisions
- **Faster questions:** WhatsApp for quick back-and-forth
- **Review meetings:** scheduled calls only when needed (no standing meetings)
- **Response time:** under 24 hours during business days
- **Working hours:** weekdays, Pakistan time

## What clients are expected to provide

- Brand guidelines (if any exist)
- Logo and brand assets
- Finalized copy by the design phase (or budget for copywriting as an add-on)
- One primary point of contact for decisions
- Timely feedback during review rounds (within 3 business days)

## Scoping questions used during discovery

When talking to a new lead, these questions help size and price the work:

1. What does your business do, and who's the customer?
2. What's the current site situation? (Existing? Outdated? Doesn't exist?)
3. What needs to happen for this site to be a success in 6 months?
4. Who owns the decision-making? (One person? Multiple stakeholders?)
5. Timeline? Hard deadline or flexible?
6. Brand guidelines and assets — ready, or do we start from scratch?
7. Copy — do you have it, or do we need to write it together?
8. Integrations needed? (Calendly, payments, CRM, etc.)
9. Budget range — even rough?

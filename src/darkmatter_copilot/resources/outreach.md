# Cold Outreach Email Structure

How Dark Matter Studio writes its cold outreach emails. Use this structure
when composing outreach to new leads who haven't engaged yet.

## Goal

Earn 30 seconds of attention. Get the lead to reply or accept a call.
Not to close the deal in the email itself.

## Structure (4 steps, 4-6 sentences total, under 120 words)

### 1. Personalization (1-2 sentences)

Open with something specific to *their* business — a recent piece of work,
a specific page on their site, what stood out about their offering.

This is not flattery. It's evidence you actually looked. If you can't write
this part honestly, don't send the email.

### 2. Problem framing (1 sentence)

Name ONE specific observable issue or opportunity on their current site.
Frame it as opportunity, not criticism.

Pre-step: if the lead has a website URL, fetch and analyze it using your
own web tools. Identify ONE observable issue. AVOID claims requiring 
measurement (load times, SEO rankings, conversion rates) — only mention
what can be seen in the page itself.

Examples of OK observations:
- "Your booking flow takes 4 clicks before showing availability"
- "There's no clear CTA above the fold"
- "Your About page tells the story but no testimonials anchor it"
- "Mobile menu has overflow issues on smaller screens"

### 3. Proof (1-2 sentences)

Reference ONE relevant past project, by name, with a specific outcome and
live URL. Pick the case study most analogous to the lead's business.

Example: "We solved this for [past client] — they now [outcome]. You can
see it at [URL]."

### 4. Low-friction CTA (1 sentence)

Ask for something small. Not "let's hop on a 30-minute call" — instead
"would it be worth 10 minutes to talk about whether something similar
could help?"

## What to avoid

- No pricing mentioned in cold outreach
- No scope detail or proposal language
- No timeline commitments
- No "I'm Saad, I've been doing this for X years" — they don't care yet
- No measurement claims that aren't substantiated
- No buzzwords ("leverage", "synergy", "transformative", "best-in-class")
- No mass-mail signal ("Hi {{first_name}}!", "I noticed your company...")

## Subject line

Specific, low-stakes, curiosity-piquing. Not "Quick question" — anything 
too generic gets filtered. Better:
- "[Specific thing about their site/business]"  
- "Saw [specific page] — one thought"
- "[Business name] + [past client]?"

Keep it under 50 characters.

# Dark Matter Studio — Positioning & Voice

## What Dark Matter does

Custom websites for small service-based businesses — professionals, creatives,
and boutique operators who need their site to be a credibility engine and a
direct conversion channel.

Not generalists. Specifically: businesses where the owner *is* the brand, and
the site needs to communicate trust, capability, and a clear next step.

## Target clients

- **Professional services**: accountants, lawyers, consultants, agencies
- **Creative professionals**: photographers, designers, writers
- **Boutique operators**: small hospitality businesses, single-product brands, 
  niche specialists
- **SaaS founders**: pre-seed to early stage, needing a marketing presence
  before they have a marketing team

Not the right fit: e-commerce stores with large catalogs, heavy SaaS applications,
enterprise corporate sites.

## Voice

The core positioning: **"We don't just build websites. We build growth engines."**

The voice is:
- **Confident, not cocky.** Direct about what we do and don't do.
- **Warm, but professional.** No jargon, no agency speak, no buzzwords.
- **Outcome-focused.** Websites are tools that earn their keep — performance,
  aesthetics, and results, in that order.
- **Honest about scope.** We don't oversell; we scope clearly up front.

Avoid: marketing-speak phrases like "leverage," "synergy," "best-in-class,"
"transformative." Avoid hyperbole. Let the work speak.

## Core differentiators (use in proposals when relevant)

- **No templates.** Every site is custom-designed from scratch to reflect
  the client's brand.
- **Modern stack.** Built with Next.js, Tailwind, deployed on Vercel — fast,
  reliable, and easy to extend.
- **Performance-first.** Built to load fast and rank well, not just look pretty.
- **Real ongoing support.** We don't disappear after launch day.
- **Direct communication.** No account-manager layer between you and the
  person doing the work.

## When writing in Dark Matter's voice

- Lead with the outcome the client wants, not the features we ship
- Use short sentences when possible
- Reference the specific business type if known (e.g., "for a photography
  business," "for a chartered accountancy firm")
- Mention relevant past work by name when it sets context
- Close with a clear next step (call, scope doc, deposit)

## What to avoid

- Don't promise specific results we can't substantiate ("we'll triple your
  conversion rate")
- Don't claim outcomes from past projects we didn't measure ("Sussex saw a 
  40% increase in bookings" — only say this if it's documented and true)
- Don't compete on price. Compete on quality, clarity, and execution.
